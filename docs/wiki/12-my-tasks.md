# My Tasks

The My Tasks page is your personal command center — every task assigned to you across every project, in one place.

---

## 1. Accessing My Tasks

Click **My Tasks** in the sidebar, or navigate to `/dashboard/my-tasks`.

---

## 2. How Tasks Are Grouped

Your tasks are automatically grouped into four time-based sections:

### Today
Tasks with a due date of **today**. These are your immediate priorities.

### This Week
Tasks due within the **next 7 days** (excluding today). Plan ahead for these.

### Later
Tasks due **after this week**. These are on the horizon but not urgent.

### No Date
Tasks with **no due date** set. Review these periodically — if they're not important enough to have a deadline, they might not be important enough to keep.

---

## 3. What Each Task Shows

Every task in the list displays:

- **Title** — the task name
- **Project name** — which project it belongs to (with project color badge)
- **Status** — Todo, In Progress, In Review, or Done
- **Priority** — Low, Medium, High, or Urgent (with color indicator)
- **Due date** — when it's due (highlighted red if overdue)
- **Blocked indicator** — visible if the task is blocked

---

## 4. Filtering My Tasks

Use the filter bar at the top to narrow down your view:

| Filter | Options |
|--------|---------|
| **Status** | Todo, In Progress, In Review, Done |
| **Priority** | Low, Medium, High, Urgent |
| **Project** | Any project you're a member of |

Filters combine with AND logic — selecting "High" priority and "In Progress" status shows only high-priority in-progress tasks.

---

## 5. Interacting with Tasks

### Opening a Task

Click any task to open the **task detail slide-out panel** on the right. From there you can:

- Edit the title, description, status, priority, or due date
- Reassign the task
- Add comments
- Flag or unflag blockers
- View the task's activity history

### Quick Actions

Without opening the detail panel:

- **Status change** — click the status badge directly to cycle through statuses
- **Complete** — check the completion checkbox (if visible)

---

## 6. Using Your PA with My Tasks

Your PA is aware of your task list. Try these:

> "What are my tasks for today?"
> "What's my most urgent task?"
> "Show me my overdue tasks"
> "What's on my plate this week?"
> "Which of my tasks are blocked?"

The PA will give you a natural language summary of your tasks, often with suggestions on what to tackle first.

---

## 7. Tips

1. **Check My Tasks first thing** — it's a faster way to see your priorities than scrolling through individual projects
2. **Use the PA for a verbal summary** — "What should I focus on?" is faster than scanning a list
3. **Clean up "No Date" tasks regularly** — either set a deadline or consider whether they still need to exist
4. **Filter by "In Progress"** to see what you're actively working on
5. **Drag status changes** aren't available here (unlike the Kanban board) — use the detail panel or quick status click instead
